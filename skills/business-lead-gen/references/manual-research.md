# Manual Research Guide

When automated tools aren't available, use this manual approach to find businesses without websites.

## Google Maps Method (Recommended)

### Step 1: Search
1. Go to https://www.google.com/maps
2. Search: `"[industry] in [city]"` (e.g., "restaurants in Brooklyn NY")
3. Switch to list view

### Step 2: Identify Targets
Look for businesses that:
- ❌ Have NO website link in their profile
- ✅ Have phone number listed
- ✅ Have reviews (shows they're active)
- ✅ Have photos (shows they care about presentation)

### Step 3: Extract Information
For each business, record:
- Business name
- Full address
- Phone number
- Email (if listed, sometimes in photos or posts)
- Google Maps URL
- Industry/category
- Number of reviews (indicates activity level)

### Step 4: Verify No Website
1. Click on the business listing
2. Look for "Website" button
3. If absent, it's a target
4. Double-check by Googling: `"[business name] [city]"`

## Quick Validation Checklist

Before adding to lead list:
- [ ] No website found
- [ ] Phone number exists
- [ ] Business appears active (recent reviews/photos)
- [ ] Contact info is visible
- [ ] Not a chain/franchise (they usually have corporate sites)
- [ ] Local independent business

## Red Flags (Skip These)

- Already has a website
- Part of large chain
- Permanently closed
- No contact information
- Spam/fake listings
- Home-based businesses (harder to reach)

## Data Entry Template

Copy this format for your CSV:

```csv
business_name,address,phone,email,industry,location,google_maps_url,has_website,notes
Business Name,123 Street City State,(555) 123-4567,,industry,City,https://maps.google.com/...,False,Notes about business
```

## Efficiency Tips

1. **Work in batches** - Focus on one neighborhood at a time
2. **Use multiple tabs** - Open several business listings at once
3. **Set a timer** - 25 minutes research, 5 minute break
4. **Target specific areas** - Start with commercial districts
5. **Check Yelp too** - Cross-reference with Yelp for more info

## Finding Email Addresses

If email isn't listed on Google Maps:

1. **Check Facebook** - Many businesses list email on their FB page
2. **Check Instagram** - Bio often has contact info
3. **Call and ask** - "I'd like to send you some information, what's the best email?"
4. **Use pattern guessing** - info@, contact@, hello@ + domain (if they have one)
5. **Hunter.io** - Free tier for finding business emails

## Sample Research Session

**Goal:** Find 20 restaurants in Brooklyn without websites

**Time needed:** ~30 minutes

**Process:**
1. Search "restaurants Brooklyn NY" on Google Maps
2. Scroll through results
3. Click on each listing
4. Check for website button
5. If no website → record details
6. Continue until you have 20

**Expected results:** 15-25% of small restaurants won't have websites

## After Research

1. Clean up your CSV (remove duplicates, fix formatting)
2. Verify phone numbers are valid
3. Try to find email addresses
4. Prioritize by:
   - Number of reviews (more = more successful)
   - Recent activity (recent reviews = still operating)
   - Quality of photos (care about appearance)

5. Start with top 10-20 for your first email campaign
